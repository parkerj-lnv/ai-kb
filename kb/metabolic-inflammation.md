## What it is

Metabolic inflammation (chronic low-grade inflammation) is a state where the immune system maintains a persistently elevated inflammatory baseline — not enough to feel acutely ill, but enough to disrupt hormonal signaling and metabolic function over time.

It differs from acute inflammation, which is the beneficial short-term response to injury or infection. The goal is not zero inflammation — some is necessary for survival — but a low, healthy baseline.

## How it disrupts metabolism

- **Insulin signaling**: Inflammatory cytokines interfere with insulin receptor sensitivity → cells become insulin-resistant → fat storage is favored over fat burning
- **Fat-burning hormones**: Inflammation blunts leptin and adiponectin signaling, reducing effective lipolysis
- **Water retention**: Inflammation causes fluid retention; reducing it produces rapid early weight loss that is real but not purely fat
- **Visceral fat cycle**: Visceral (organ) fat both produces and is worsened by inflammation — a self-reinforcing loop

## Key inflammatory markers

| Marker | What it measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| IL-6 | Pro-inflammatory cytokine elevated in chronic inflammation |
| TNF-alpha | Promotes insulin resistance; marker of inflammatory state |
| CRP | C-reactive protein; broad-spectrum inflammation indicator |
| Waist circumference | Practical clinical proxy for visceral fat / inflammatory load |

## Relationship to olive oil

High-polyphenol [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]] — specifically its [[oleocanthal]] content — has been shown to reduce IL-6 and TNF-alpha in people with metabolic syndrome (2020 study, 2 months). Reductions in these markers accompanied:
- Reduced waist circumference
- Improved visceral fat ratio
- Improved fatty liver index

See also [[predimed-study]] for population-level evidence linking EVOO consumption to improved waist circumference vs. low-fat diets.

## Sleep and cortisol connection

Reducing inflammation also reduces cortisol (the stress hormone). Chronically elevated cortisol — which inflammation can drive — disrupts sleep architecture. Lowering the inflammatory load may improve deep sleep and REM sleep, which supports further metabolic recovery. (Plausible mechanism; formal studies linking EVOO specifically to sleep are limited as of 2026.)
