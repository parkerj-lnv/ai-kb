# PREDIMED Study

#health

**Summary**: PREDIMED (Prevención con Dieta Mediterránea) is one of the largest nutrition trials ever run, demonstrating that a Mediterranean diet supplemented with extra virgin olive oil outperforms a low-fat diet for fat loss, waist circumference reduction, and cardiovascular risk.

## Study arms

1. **Mediterranean diet + 4 tbsp [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]]/day**
2. Mediterranean diet + mixed nuts
3. Low-fat control diet

## Key findings

- The olive oil group lost **more fat** than the low-fat control group — directly contradicting the "fat makes you fat" premise
- The olive oil group showed **greater reductions in waist circumference** — a reliable proxy for visceral fat / [[metabolic-inflammation]]
- Cardiovascular outcomes also favored the olive oil and nuts groups over the low-fat group

## Significance

PREDIMED is a frequently cited landmark in nutrition science because:
- Large-scale, long-duration, and reasonably well-controlled
- The olive oil dose (4 tbsp/day) is higher than most people consume, suggesting most individuals are under-dosing for meaningful metabolic effect
- Results held even though the Mediterranean diet group was consuming more total fat than the control group

## Limitations and caveats

- The video source (Ben Azadi) is a sponsored content creator — findings are cited accurately but selectively emphasized to support a product pitch
- PREDIMED faced methodology scrutiny: some randomization concerns led to a partial retraction and re-analysis in 2018. The core conclusions held in the re-analysis, but the study is not without controversy.
