## Overview

Ben Azadi (metabolic health content creator) documented a personal 10-day experiment consuming 3 tablespoons of [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]] per day — taken as shots between meals — with no other changes to diet or training. **Note**: the video ends with a sponsored pitch for "Fresh Pressed Olive Oil Club"; claims should be weighed with that context in mind.

## Studies cited

- **2005 Nature study (Monell Chemical Senses Center)**: Identified [[oleocanthal]] in fresh EVOO; shown to act on COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes the same way ibuprofen does.
- **[[predimed-study]]**: 7,447-person Spanish trial — Mediterranean diet + 4 tbsp EVOO/day group lost more fat and had smaller waist circumference than a low-fat control group.
- **Frontiers of Nutrition study**: 16,273 adults — those consuming 2+ tbsp EVOO ≥6 days/week averaged a 35 in waist vs. 39 in for irregular consumers (4-inch difference).
- **2020 metabolic syndrome study**: 23 people, 2 months of high-oleocanthal EVOO — IL-6, TNF-alpha, waist circumference, visceral fat ratio, and fatty liver index all improved.
- **UC Davis 2010 study**: Tested 124 imported EVOO bottles sold in California — most failed international authenticity standards (diluted, rancid, or oxidized). FDA has contested the rate, but quality variation is real.

## Mechanisms

- **Postprandial glucose**: 10g EVOO with a meal cut post-meal blood sugar spikes by 20%+ in one study — reduces the insulin/cravings roller coaster
- **Satiety hormones**: Triggers CCK, peptide YY, and leptin (GLP-1 signaling pathway) — the same pathways targeted by Ozempic/semaglutide, activated naturally by fat
- **Gastric emptying**: EVOO slows it — beneficial for blood sugar stability but problematic when stacked on top of an already large meal
- **[[Metabolic-inflammation]]**: Reducing inflammation allows fat-burning hormones to signal effectively; also reduces water retention
- **Thermogenesis**: EVOO may increase diet-induced thermogenesis by ~5.1% vs. other diets
- **Sleep/cortisol**: Reduced inflammation → lower cortisol → improved sleep quality (Azadi's hypothesis; formal studies limited)

## Olive oil quality

Most supermarket EVOO is suspect — possibly oxidized, past its polyphenol window, or adulterated with cheaper oils. The peppery throat burn when drinking straight is the key field indicator of [[oleocanthal]] presence; smooth oil is a red flag.
