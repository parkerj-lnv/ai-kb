## Core principle

Benefits from [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]] at therapeutic doses depend heavily on **how** it is taken, not just how much. Spacing doses between meals — not with food — lets satiety hormones fire without competing with active digestion.

## Full protocol (3 tbsp/day)

| Dose | Timing | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 tbsp | Morning, empty stomach | Follow with a full glass of water |
| 1 tbsp | Mid-afternoon, between meals | Do not stack on top of food |
| 1 tbsp | Early evening, before dinner | Blunts appetite going into the meal |

## Ramp-up for beginners

Jumping straight to 3 tbsp/day can cause GI discomfort (bloating, heaviness) as the gallbladder and gut microbiome adapt. A gradual ramp avoids this:

| Week | Dose |
|------|------|
| 1 | 1 tbsp/day — morning on empty stomach |
| 2 | 2 tbsp/day — morning + mid-afternoon |
| 3+ | Full 3 tbsp/day protocol |

## The critical mistake to avoid

Do **not** take multiple doses immediately after a large meal. EVOO significantly slows gastric emptying — beneficial for blood sugar in normal use, but produces a "brick in the stomach" sensation when stacked on already-digesting food. Azadi took 2 tbsp right after a heavy steak/sweet potato dinner on Day 6 and felt significant discomfort within 40 minutes.

## Mechanisms this protocol activates

- **Satiety hormones**: CCK, peptide YY, leptin — GLP-1 signaling pathway, same pathway as semaglutide (Ozempic), triggered naturally
- **Postprandial glucose control**: Reduces post-meal blood sugar spike by 20%+ (10g dose, per one study)
- **[[Oleocanthal]] absorption**: Taken without competing macronutrients for cleaner absorption
- **Gastric emptying**: Slowed beneficially when taken before a meal; problematic after

## Oil quality requirement

Only high-polyphenol [[extra-virgin-olive-oil]] produces these effects. Using cheap, oxidized, or adulterated supermarket EVOO will not work and may be counterproductive:

- Must produce a strong peppery throat burn and mild cough when sipped straight
- Cold-pressed, single-origin, early harvest preferred
- Dark glass bottle; check harvest date
